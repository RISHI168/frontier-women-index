# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

The Frontier Women Index exists to document women's contributions to frontier science and technology with rigor and respect. The spaces associated with this project — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any affiliated channels — are committed to being places where anyone who contributes in good faith is treated with dignity, regardless of their background, experience level, or identity.

This Code of Conduct applies to all contributors, maintainers, and readers who interact with the project.

## Expected behavior

- **Assume good faith.** Most disagreements are about the work, not the people. Treat them that way.
- **Focus on accuracy and evidence.** This is a research project. Claims should be supported, disagreements should reference sources, and updates should improve the record.
- **Credit contributions.** Acknowledge the work of others when building on it.
- **Be patient with newcomers.** First-time contributors may not know the methodology yet. Point them to it kindly.
- **Correct errors gracefully.** Everyone makes mistakes, including maintainers. A correction is a collaboration, not a rebuke.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following will not be tolerated:

- **Harassment of any kind**, including but not limited to: sexualized comments or imagery, personal insults, trolling, deliberate intimidation, stalking, or unwelcome attention.
- **Dismissiveness of the project's focus.** Comments suggesting that documenting women's contributions is unnecessary, tokenistic, or discriminatory will be removed. Discussion of methodology (how we include, how we tier, how we source) is welcome; attempts to undermine the project's existence are not.
- **Attacks on any contributor's identity**, including national origin, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability status, or age.
- **Bad-faith nominations or corrections** designed to manipulate the Index rather than improve it. This includes coordinated inauthentic behavior.
- **Sharing private information** about contributors or listed leaders without their consent.
- **Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional research setting.**

## Special considerations for this project

Because the Index documents real people, contributors have special responsibilities:

- **Do not use the Index to target listed leaders.** Their inclusion is based on public professional contributions, not an invitation to scrutinize their personal lives.
- **Raise misconduct allegations through the established process** (see [`METHODOLOGY.md`](./METHODOLOGY.md#misconduct-allegations)), not in issues or PRs.
- **Do not doxx** listed leaders or contributors. Personal contact information beyond professional institutional channels should never be added to the Index.

## Enforcement

Violations may be reported to the maintainers at `hello@frontierwomenindex.org` or through GitHub's private vulnerability reporting. Reports are treated confidentially.

Maintainers will review and respond within 7 days. Responses may include:

- A private warning with clarification of expected behavior
- A public warning referenced in the relevant issue or PR
- Temporary suspension from contributing
- Permanent ban from all project spaces
- Reversal of prior contributions if made in bad faith

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and when an individual is representing the project in public, including on social media.
