## Becoming a maintainer

Maintainership is offered, not applied for. We look for contributors who have:

- Verified 20+ entries to the `verified` standard
- Authored at least one research report or significant methodology contribution
- Demonstrated sustained engagement over 6+ months
- Committed to the project's editorial independence and methodology

Maintainers are added by consensus of existing maintainers and announced in `CHANGELOG.md`.

## Maintainer conduct

Maintainers are held to the same [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) as contributors, with additional expectations:

- Declare conflicts of interest proactively when reviewing entries about leaders they have relationships with
- Do not be the sole reviewer for any entry where a conflict is declared
- Treat corrections as neutral information, not as attacks
- Maintain the editorial bar regardless of outside pressure

## Stepping down

A maintainer may step down at any time by opening an issue. Their name remains in `CONTRIBUTORS.md` (archived) with the dates of their active maintainership. Entries they previously verified retain their names as reviewers.

## Governance disputes

If maintainers disagree on a substantive editorial decision, the process is:

1. Discussion in a private maintainer channel
2. If no consensus, the decision goes to public discussion on GitHub
3. If still no consensus, the decision is deferred until new evidence emerges or a wider community vote is held (for methodology-scale disputes)

Editorial independence from the founding sponsor is non-negotiable. If TechFondue or any future sponsor attempts to influence an editorial decision, this is disclosed publicly in this file and in `CORRECTIONS.md`.
